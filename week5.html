Chemical Reaction

Synthesis / Combination

Decomposition

Substitution / Single Replacement

Metathesis / Double Replacement

Reaction that involves combining at least two (2) elements together to form a compound

Equation: A + B → AB

Example: 2Na+ (s) + Cl2 (g) → 2NaCl (s)

Reaction that involves breaking down a compound into its base constituents, or it involves combination, but releases some base constituents as by-products.

AB → A + B or A + B → AB + base constituent

Example: 2H2O (l) → 2H2 (g) + O2 (g)

Reaction involving two (2) substances, wherein one substance takes the place of one of the constituents of the compound, forming a new compound without decomposing.

Equation: AB + C → AC + B

Example: 2HNO3 + 2 Ag+ → 2AgNO3 + H2(g)

Reaction between two (2) compounds that switches one of their constituents over the other, forming new substances in their own right.

Equation: AB + CD → AD + CB

Example: HIO + NaNO2 → NaIO + HNO2

Whenever chemical bonds between atoms are established or broken, a chemical reaction is initiated. The chemicals that are introduced into a chemical reaction are referred to as the reactants, and the compounds that are created at the conclusion of the reaction are referred to as the products of the reaction.
